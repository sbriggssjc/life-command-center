## R66c — Bid-Ask redesigned to dual-axis (2026-06-01)

The master (`chart7`) is two dash-marker series with **no gray band**, but it plots
Last-Ask cap (~5–8%) and the raw Bid-Ask Spread (~0.2–0.8%) on a *single* cap axis,
so the spread line falls off the bottom and is invisible. Per your call, rebuilt the
chart **dual-axis** (new `buildBidAskDualAxisChartXml` for native Excel — two
`lineChart` groups sharing the cat axis, each on its own valAx; mirrored with
`comboOpts` in the PNG/PDF renderer; both validated):

- **Left axis (Sky dash):** Last Ask cap — 5.25–8% (dia) / 5.5–10% (gov)
- **Right axis (Navy dash):** Bid-Ask Spread — own scale (−1.5% to +2%)
- **Gray dispersion band removed.**

**Data caveat to decide on:** our spread is *signed* (`sold − last_ask`), which we made
signed in round-66 to satisfy your "last ask + spread = sold cap" identity. For
dialysis that lands mostly **near zero / slightly negative** (p50 −0.02%, bulk
−1.2%…+0.3%) — i.e. dia caps barely move ask→sale — whereas the master's "Bid-Ask
Spread" is a clean **positive 0.2–0.8%**. So our navy line will hover around zero, not
sit at +0.5% like the master. That's a data/definition difference, not a chart bug. If
you want it to read like the master, the options are (a) plot the **absolute** spread
magnitude, or (b) revisit the underlying ask/sold cap data for dia. Say which and I'll
apply it. (Code-only change — needs deploy + fresh export to view.)
