The headline. The plumbing (sources → DBs → API → UI) is mature and largely works. The problem is the last mile: enormous volumes of review-ready work sit in database views with no proportionate, consolidated UI home — and the system's own health (failing jobs, dead workers) has no surface at all. Information architecture — current vs proposed

The current IA is organized by domain (Gov vs Dialysis), which fragments identical review work into parallel, hard-to-find places. The proposed IA adds a work-type spine: a single Review Console and an Ops Health surface, so a reviewer goes to one place for all conflicts/merges/research regardless of domain.

Current

  • Today
  • KPIs · Next Best Action · briefing · weather · markets
  • Dialysis (11–14 inner tabs)
  • Overview · Search · Properties · CMS Data · Inventory Changes · NPI Intel · Sales · Leases · Loans · Players
  • Research (6 modes) — quarantine, unmatched, clarification, property, lease, clinic_leads, staleness
  • Prospects · Activity (listing verification buried here) · Capital Markets
  • Gov (11 inner tabs)
  • Overview · Search · Ownership · Pipeline · Sales · Prospects · Leases · Loans · Players
  • Research (6 modes) — pending_updates, pipeline_control, ownership, leads, intel, financial_overrides
  • Capital Markets
  • Pipeline

  • Inbox

  • More drawer (flat, 10 items)
  • Contacts · Business · Calendar · Messages · Entities · Research · Metrics · Sync Health · Data Quality · Settings
  • Property Detail (slide-over, 6 tabs)

  • Keep domain Overview/Search/Sales/Leases/Players; route the 6 "Research modes" into the shared Review Console (deep-link, domain pre-filtered)
  • Workspaces group (drawer → grouped)
  • Work: Pipeline · Inbox · Review Console
  • Intelligence: Contacts · Entities · Capital Markets · Metrics
  • Admin: Sync Health · Ops Health · Settings
  • Property Detail elevate
  • Persistent "Needs review" banner surfacing provenance conflicts + merge candidates (today buried in tabs/Consolidate button)
Why a work-type spine. Today a reviewer resolving ownership data must hop between Gov→Research→ownership, Dialysis→Research→property, the Property-detail Ownership&CRM tab, and the standalone Research page — four contexts for one job. A single Review Console with domain filters collapses that to one. The redesign mockups (separate file) show the lane layout.

Live ops health — invisible to the app today

These were caught by querying the health/alert views during the audit. None of them surface anywhere a human would see them in the LCC UI, so they have been silently degrading. This is the single strongest argument for a top-level Ops Health surface.

Dialysis auto-merge — failing hourly 24h+

auto_merge_property_failures fires every hour. FK violation: can't delete losing property because sales_transactions rows aren't reparented first. ~24 open warn alerts, one new each hour.

Gov nightly hygiene sweep — partially broken

data_hygiene_sweep_step_error: tries REFRESH MATERIALIZED VIEW on v_sales_comps & v_available_listings, which are plain VIEWS. Two steps error every night.

LLC/SOS research stalled — 8+ days

research_queue_stalled open on both dia (1,304) & gov (664). 0 completed — no worker drains the queue (deferred pending free SOS scraper).

LCC — flow failure + write-failure pile-up

Open flow_failure: PA "SF → LCC Daily Bulk File Backfill" failed at Apply-to-each. ingest_write_failures at 10,577 rows / 2,676 recent.

Provenance governance un-deployed

field_source_priority = 8 rows live vs 462 documented → 37 unranked-field drift rows and 12,332 actionable conflicts ungoverned.

Listing verification falling behind

Gov: 200 listings never verified, 126 overdue 30d. Dia: 74 never verified, 26 overdue. Probe cadence outruns capacity.

Recommendation. Add an Ops Health surface fed by v_cron_health_summary, v_lcc_health_alerts_open, v_flow_run_failures_open and the two domain v_data_quality_summary views, plus an "open alerts" badge on Today. Two of the six issues above (auto-merge, hygiene sweep) are also code bugs with clear fixes noted in the report.
