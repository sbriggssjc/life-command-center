Life Command Center — Self-Propelling UX Audit

Holistic re-audit · organizational layout + interaction · 2026-06-03
The north star: the flow of data and prospecting should follow the way work actually moves, so the user always knows where they are, what to do next, and every interaction advances the ball forward for that property or prospect — the app should feel like it is self-propelling the work, always surfacing the next best action.

That north star decomposes into three properties the app must have. This audit grades each, then ranks the moves that close the gaps. The BD spine itself is now continuous (prior work); what remains is making the whole app behave the way the spine does.

WEAK

The target: one "Next Best Action" contract every surface honors

The fix isn't 30 bespoke tweaks — it's one repeated pattern. Every work surface and every property/prospect should obey the same contract, so the behavior becomes predictable and the app teaches itself:

  • Lead with ONE action. The single highest-value next step is visually primary (one shared button style), above everything else.
  • Show the priority. The top item is visually elevated — "work this first" is never ambiguous.
  • Advance on completion. Acting decrements the remaining count and routes/points to the next item — never a dead stop.
  • Every record offers a forward move. Even reference surfaces give at least one "do the next thing" (open property / start outreach / create task).
  • Per-entity progress. Each property/prospect shows where it is in the spine and the next push, updating as you act.

Ranked moves

Ranked by impact on the north star (self-propulsion), smallest-first within tiers. Badges map each move to the property it strengthens.

DirectionMomentum1 · The universal Next-Best-Action banner + auto-advance on queues

Surfaces: Priority Queue, Review Console, Research, My Work · Effort: M

Elevate item #1 on every queue ("work this first" styling), and on completion decrement a visible remaining counter and advance to / highlight the next item. This single pattern is the heart of "self-propelling." Build once, apply to all four queues.

Direction2 · Property "Next step for this property" banner

Surface: property detail (top, above tabs) · Effort: S–M

A single resolved banner at the top of every property that names the one next action for it (resolve owner → confirm link → create lead → add cadence) and shows remaining steps — pulling from the data the ladder/feed already compute, but surfaced where the eye lands first and updating as you act.

Orientation3 · Promote Priority Queue to a primary destination

Surface: bottom nav / Today · Effort: S

The "always work the top" front door shouldn't live in the drawer. Make it a first-class entry (bottom-nav slot or the anchor of the Today landing) so the prioritized next action is one tap from anywhere.

Momentum4 · Make reference surfaces active

Surfaces: Contacts, Entities · Effort: M

Give every contact/entity at least one forward action — Open property, Start outreach / add to cadence, Create research task — so lookups become launch points instead of dead-ends.

SubstrateDirection5 · Unify the action + card vocabulary

Surfaces: global (styles.css + renderers) · Effort: M (incremental)

Collapse the 8+ primary-button variants into one .btn-primary and the 6+ row patterns into one .card-row with modifiers. This is what makes "the next action" recognizable everywhere — the substrate that lets moves 1–4 read consistently. Roll in incrementally as each surface is touched.

Orientation6 · Resolve the IA confusions

Surfaces: nav (Business/Dia/Gov, Inbox/Messages) · Effort: S

Make the drawer "Business" entry honor the domain (or retire it in favor of the Dia/Gov shortcuts), and disambiguate Inbox (triage) vs Messages (archive) by label. Removes the "two doors, different behavior" friction.

Recommended first move: #1 (the Next-Best-Action banner + auto-advance). It delivers the most of the north star — "always prioritizing the next best action" and "self-propelling forward" — and once built, the pattern is reused by moves 2–4. Move 5 (the unified vocabulary) rides along incrementally as each surface is touched, so the app converges on coherence without a big-bang refactor.
Sources: three code/IA traces of the live LCC frontend (navigation & IA; per-surface next-action; interaction & visual consistency), 2026-06-03. Grades are relative to the self-propelling north star, not absolute quality — the app is well-built; the gap is in flow continuity across surfaces.
