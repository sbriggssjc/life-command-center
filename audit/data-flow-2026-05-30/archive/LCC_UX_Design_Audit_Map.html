LCC UX & Design Audit — does the app's look, order & feel match the data flow?

The fifth audit lens: visual hierarchy, navigation, element ordering vs. data flow, interaction & state design, and component/brand consistency.
2026-05-31 · Live walkthrough (Today/Gov/Dia/Property Detail) + styles.css & index.html structural read · Graded A–F per dimension

Design dimension grades

Each dimension scored against the goal: the app's appearance, interaction, order and structure should mirror how the data flows and how a deal actually progresses, so the UI is self-evidently intuitive.

Visual hierarchy C

Clean dark theme, but the eye doesn't travel in task order — KPIs, NBA, My Work and Inbox compete for primacy with no clear "do this first."

Navigation coherence D

Four nesting levels (bottom nav → domain top-tabs → inner pills → sub-tabs) plus a flat 10-item More drawer. Users must know where things live.

Order vs data flow C

Property detail roughly tracks the lifecycle but front-loads Overview and buries Ownership (the payoff) at tab 4, prospecting inside it.

Interaction & state D

14.6s loads, screenshot-freezing renders, pervasive empty states ("not on file"), inconsistent loading/skeleton treatment.

Component & brand consistency D

~1,000 inline styles in one file; brand hierarchy inverted (Sky used as primary, NM Blue as secondary); body font is system stack, not brand.

Overall: C− / D+. The app is functional and information-dense, but it is organized for the system's convenience (by domain, by table) rather than the user's task flow (find a deal → understand it → identify the owner → act). The structure audit (lens 1) proposed the right re-org; this lens says the visual & interaction execution needs to follow, or the re-org alone won't feel intuitive.

Navigation depth — the four-level problem

To reach a government property's ownership data today, a user passes through four distinct navigation mechanisms. Each is a place they must learn.

LEVEL 1Bottom nav: Today · Dialysis · Gov · Pipeline · Inbox · More ▾tap 1
LEVEL 2Domain top-tabs: Dialysis · Government · National ST · Marketing · Prospects · All Othertap 2
LEVEL 3Inner pills: Overview · Pipeline · Research · Reference · Capital Marketstap 3
LEVEL 4Sub-tabs: Overview · Search · CMS Data · NPI Inteltap 4
+DRAWERMore drawer (flat 10): Contacts · Business · Calendar · Messages · Entities · Research · Metrics · Sync Health · Data Quality · Settingsparallel
The two concrete problems. (1) Research lives in three places at once — a Level-3 inner pill in both Gov and Dia, AND a top-level item in the More drawer — so the same job has three doors and no single home. (2) The More drawer is a flat list of 10 where Data Quality and Research (operational, high-frequency) rank visually identical to Settings (rare). Nothing signals importance or grouping.

What good looks like

Collapse to three coherent groups and a single home per job: Work (Pipeline · Inbox · Review Console), Intelligence (Contacts · Entities · Capital Markets · Metrics), Admin (Sync Health · Ops Health · Settings) — with the domain dashboards kept for browsing, not as the entry point for review work.

Element order vs. the data flow

The property-intelligence flow doc defined the natural lifecycle order. The live property detail mostly follows it but front-loads a generic Overview and buries the payoff. Reordering to match the flow makes the panel self-guiding.

Live property-detail tab order

  1. Overview (generic dump — identity + pipeline + financials mixed)
  2. Rent Roll (lease / tenancy)
  3. Operations (agency / operator)
  4. Ownership & CRM ← the payoff, buried at #4
  5. Deal History (sales)
  6. Activity Log

Prospecting actions live inside the Ownership tab and at the bottom of the panel, not as a destination.

Lifecycle / data-flow order (proposed)

  1. Identity & snapshot (who/what/where + the "needs review" banner)
  2. Tenancy & terms (lease)
  3. Operation & agency
  4. Market activity & value (sales / cap rate)
  5. Ownership de-anonymization ← elevated; the ladder is the spine
  6. Prospecting convergence ← a real destination, not buried
  7. Activity log

Order mirrors the flow: identify the asset → understand its economics → resolve who owns it → act. Each tab ends by pointing at the next.

The Phase 4 ladder & Phase 8 feed already shipped — but they sit inside the old tab order. Reordering the panel so they're the climax (not a mid-list tab + a footer) is the design change that makes the data flow legible at a glance.

Proposed design direction

Five moves, ordered by how much "intuitive-ness" they buy. None require re-plumbing data — they re-order and re-skin what exists so the surface matches the flow.

#MoveWhat it fixesEffort
1Re-order property detail to lifecycle order + elevate Ownership & Prospecting as the climaxOrder-vs-flow; makes the panel self-guiding without new dataS–M
2Collapse navigation: group the More drawer into Work / Intelligence / Admin; give Review one homeNavigation coherence (D→B); kills the 3-doors-for-Research problemM
3Fix brand hierarchy: NM Blue primary, Sky accent; load brand font for body; standardize on tokenized component classesConsistency/brand (D→B); ~1,000 inline styles → shared classesM–L
4Establish a state system: consistent skeleton loaders, a single empty-state pattern ("not on file → here's the action"), virtualize 5k-row rendersInteraction/state (D→B); kills the 14.6s freezesM
5Add a "do this first" visual hierarchy on Today: one primary surface (Review/NBA), demote KPIs to a stripVisual hierarchy (C→B)S
Sequencing note. #1 and #5 are quick wins that immediately make the app feel flow-aligned. #2 pairs naturally with building the Review Console (already the structure audit's #1). #3 is the larger investment but it's what makes every future screen consistent — best done as a shared component pass, not per-screen.
