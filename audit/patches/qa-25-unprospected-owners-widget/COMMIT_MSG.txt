QA-25 (P2): reframe "Missing SF Link" widget into actionable "Unprospected Owners" queue

The widget read 97% missing on gov and 79% on dia. The number looked like
a data-quality alarm but was misleading on two coupled axes — and a name-
matching backfill was structurally impossible.

(a) STUB POLLUTION. The widget counted ALL true_owners, including 6,303
    gov + 2,580 dia rows that own ZERO properties (44.7% and 75.4% of
    each domain). These are residue from the LLC research queue and from
    property merges/deletes. They inflated the denominator without
    representing anything actionable.

(b) WRONG FRAME. "Missing SF Link" implies the link exists in SF and the
    join broke. It doesn't. salesforce_accounts on dia (5,004 rows) is
    Scott's CRM contact book, not an owner universe. Gov has no
    salesforce_accounts table at all. Diagnostic queries:

      - Exact lowercase-trim name matches between 2,722 unlinked dia
        owners and 5,004 SF accounts: 0
      - Top 18 unlinked owners by prop count: best pg_trgm fuzzy match
        similarity is 0.23 - 0.55 (every match is a different company)

    The owners aren't "missing a link." They're unprospected BD targets
    (SMBC Leasing 104 props, Elliott Bay Capital 65, MassMutual 57,
    Realty Income Corporation 25, Healthcare Realty Trust 7; Boyd
    Watterson Global 31, Prologis L.P. 24, Highwoods Realty 21, etc.).

Fix (omnibus):

1. New v_prospect_targets view (gov + dia): owners with >=1 property
   and no SF link, ordered by prop_count. Dia excludes is_operator_not_owner.

2. Widget reframe in gov.js + dialysis.js: "Missing SF Link" ->
   "Unprospected Owners". Numerator AND denominator filtered to active
   owners. Subtext changed to "active owners - click to view BD targets".
   Card now clickable.

3. Prospect modal: top-100 sortable list opens on click with owner name,
   property count, state, and contact status.

4. Edge Function v16 redeployed to dia project (zqzrriwuavgrquhisnoa)
   with v_prospect_targets in both GOV_READ_TABLES and DIA_READ_TABLES.

Verified live:
  - Dia view: SMBC Leasing 104 props at top
  - Gov view: Boyd Watterson Global 31 props at top

Lesson: when a dashboard metric reads like a data-quality alarm but the
underlying matching can't possibly succeed, the metric is the bug, not
the data. Reframing the widget converts the same number from a complaint
into a BD queue.
