audit(discovery-01): gov schema mirror + loans CHECK expansion

Surfaced by item #5's ingest_write_failures instrumentation within 2
minutes of going live. Three patterns of silent gov-side writes were
running on every CoStar sidebar capture for an unknown duration:

  1. upsertDomainOwners:linkOwnershipToSale  (sidebar-pipeline.js:6744)
     PATCHed ownership_history.sale_id, which existed on dia but not gov.
  2. upsertDomainOwners:linkSaleToOwner  (sidebar-pipeline.js:6684)
     PATCHed sales_transactions.recorded_owner_id + recorded_owner_name,
     both existed on dia but not gov.
  3. upsertDomainLoans:financing  (sidebar-pipeline.js:5424)
     INSERTed gov.loans with loan_type='Refinance'/'Acquisition' (output
     of mapLoanType(), built for dia's CHECK). gov's loans_loan_type_check
     only allowed bank-product values, so every loan row was rejected.

Closes 3 of 5 silent-failure patterns observed by item #5 in the first
2 minutes of being live. The remaining 2 (12x 409 dedupe on
sales_transactions + D-13 ownership_research_queue) are tracked as
separate items (Task #23 + #5 Phase B respectively).

Why this happened
-----------------

A-5-class schema drift: dia and gov are sibling Supabase projects with
their own migration lineage. A migration that added these columns + the
broader CHECK on dia was never mirrored to gov. Both drifts were invisible
because of the silent-write loop (audit finding A-3) — which item #5 just
fixed, making this discovery possible.
