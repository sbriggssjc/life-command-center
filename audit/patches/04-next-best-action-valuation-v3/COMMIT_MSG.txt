audit(item-4/v3): NOI ÷ cap_rate valuation methodology

Item #4 valuation v3. Both dia and gov v_property_value_signal views
rewritten to prefer real transaction prices, then NOI ÷ TTM cap rate
(broker methodology per Scott), then capped SF proxy, then heavily-
discounted polluted columns.

Audit-discovery that motivated this
-----------------------------------

Scott reported he sold 33820 Weyerhaeuser Way (Federal Way, WA) for
$115M; v_next_best_action ranked it at $575M. Investigation found
multiple polluted columns on dia:
  • current_value_estimate, last_known_rent, leases.annual_rent all
    store dialysis-operator BUSINESS valuations (revenue × ~5× EBITDA
    multiple), not real estate values.
  • Top-ranked properties showed implausible $/SF ($12,363/SF on a
    5,880 SF building in Maryland).
  • Same operator-revenue numbers replicated across multiple writer
    paths.

Fix
---

Per Scott's broker methodology: NOI ÷ TTM cap rate from CM reports.
NOI ≈ active NNN lease annual_rent (with $5M sanity cap to filter
the operator-revenue pollution). Cap rate from cm_dialysis_cap_ttm_q
and cm_gov_cap_by_term_q (by lease-term tier; falls back to
cap_outside_firm → cm_gov_cap_ttm_q on gov; subspecialty='all' for dia).

Value priority (both domains)
  1. Recent sale within 10y (truth)
  2. Active listing price (market)
  3. NOI ÷ cap_rate (broker methodology)
  4. SF × $400, capped at 200K (dia) / 500K (gov)
  5. estimated_value (gov) or current_value_estimate × 0.2 (dia, polluted)
  6. gross_rent × 10 (gov) or last_known_rent × 2 capped (dia, polluted)
  7. $1M baseline
