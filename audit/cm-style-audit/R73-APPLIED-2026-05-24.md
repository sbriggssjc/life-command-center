# R73 — Sentiment pre-2014 backfill (18 available_listings rows)

R72 unblocked this by populating sale_ids for the master backfill sales.
R73 wires `available_listings` rows for the pre-2014 master sales that
carry listing-side data (initial/last price + initial/last cap), so the
`seller_sentiment` view extends back further than R70's Q3-2014 effective
start.

## What landed

**18 of 53 pre-2014 candidates inserted** into `available_listings`.

Funnel:
- 53 master sales pre-2014 with listing-side data (initial+last price OR initial+last cap).
- 36 found a matching `property_id` in our DB (same matching logic as R72: state + first-number + normalized street, plus 2-3-word-prefix fuzzy fallback).
- **20** also had a matching `sale_id` in `sales_transactions` for the (property_id, sale_date, price±$5K) tuple — either pre-existing or backfilled by R72.
- **18** inserted (2 candidates skipped during execution — likely sale_id pairs missing or other constraint).

All rows tagged `notes='master_xlsx_listings_backfill_r73'` for clean rollback:
```sql
DELETE FROM available_listings WHERE notes = 'master_xlsx_listings_backfill_r73';
```

Set `is_active=false` (since these are sold listings, not active), and
`status='sold'`. The partial unique index
`available_listings_one_active_per_property` (WHERE is_active IS TRUE)
correctly only enforces uniqueness for currently-active listings — the
historical sold listings are unconstrained.

`listing_date` estimated at `sale_date − 180 days` since master doesn't
expose ON MARKET / DOM data in a structured form in this slice. The
seller_sentiment view doesn't use listing_date directly — it joins on
`sale_transaction_id` and reads `initial_price`/`last_price`/`last_cap_rate`.

## What this does NOT do (yet)

- **R70's chart trim doesn't extend backward automatically.** R70's
  `findFirstDenseYear(rows, 'n_all', 5)` requires 4 consecutive months
  at n≥5 — the 18 new rows don't quite hit that bar pre-2014 (most
  enriched cells are n=2-4). The chart still effectively starts at
  ~Q3-2014. To extend back further requires either (a) the remaining
  16 unmatched master sales to land (needs property creation) or (b)
  lowering the R70 density threshold from n≥5 to n≥3 (tradeoff:
  noisier early periods).

- **2009 and earlier**: master had ~6 pre-2009 candidates with listing
  data; only 4 had a matching sale_id and 2 had no NULL-handling
  issues. These quarters are still effectively empty (n=1-3) for
  sentiment.

## Recommended follow-up

Two options to actually move the chart's effective start backward:

1. **R70 threshold relaxation**: lower the data-aware cutoff to
   `n_all ≥ 3` (instead of ≥5). With the R73 enrichment this would
   pull the chart's start back to ~Q1-2012 for dia. Trade-off: those
   early TTMs would be more volatile (sentiment chart line jumps).

2. **R74**: backfill the 419 master sales with no matching property
   (the bigger lever). Adds property records + sale rows + (where
   master carries it) listing rows. Closes the remaining 80bps gap
   *and* lifts sentiment sample sizes further.

Both deferred to the next round.
