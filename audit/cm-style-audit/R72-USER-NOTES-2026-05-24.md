# R72 — Sales-universe reconciliation: backfill 621 missing sales

User notes 2026-05-23 batch 6 follow-up to R70 (which documented a
~80bps cap-rate gap between our charts and the master Excel). R72
traces the root cause and generates a migration to close most of it.

## Investigation

### Step 1: extract master Sales Comps (2,551 rows)

Extracted from `Dialysis Comp Work MASTER.xlsx > Sales Comps` with
columns: DATE (S), SOLD CAP (R), SOLD PRICE (P), TENANT (B), ADDRESS
(C), CITY (D), STATE (E), SELLER (T), BUYER (V), INITIAL PRICE (AA),
INITIAL CAP (AB), LAST PRICE (AD), LAST CAP (AE).

### Step 2: match master → our sales_transactions (2,665 rows)

Match keys, in order: (sale_date, normalized-address), (sale_date,
sold_price-to-nearest-$1K), (sale_date ± 30d, normalized-address),
(sale_date ± 90d, sold_price).

| Bucket | Count |
| --- | ---: |
| Master rows with parseable date | 2,550 |
| Master → ours (matched) | 1,510 |
| Master → ours (UNMATCHED — "master has, we don't") | **1,040** |
| Ours → master (matched) | 1,430 |
| Ours → master (UNMATCHED — "we have, master doesn't") | 1,235 |

### Step 3: which side is responsible for the 80bps gap?

Reconstructed TTM cap rates using:
- (a) all our sales
- (b) only-our-sales-that-matched-master

| Period | Master | Ours (all) | Ours (matched-only) |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| 2009-Q1 | 9.00% (n=16) | 7.87% (n=18) | 7.72% (n=8) |
| 2010-Q2 | 9.17% (n=18) | 8.32% (n=14) | 8.54% (n=10) |
| 2011-Q4 | 8.29% (n=45) | 8.25% (n=45) | 8.39% (n=24) |
| 2013-Q4 | 7.74% (n=70) | 7.41% (n=50) | 7.47% (n=29) |
| 2017-Q4 | 6.61% (n=191) | 6.48% (n=138) | 6.49% (n=99) |

**Insight**: matched-only and unmatched-ours have nearly identical avg
caps (6.76% vs 6.80% overall). So our extra sales are NOT pulling the
average down disproportionately. The gap is on the OTHER side —
**master has 1,040 sales we never ingested**, many at higher cap rates,
and they pull master's average up.

| 2011 | 12 | 8.67% |

| 2015 | 44 | 7.73% |

Master's "missing-from-us" 2010 sales have an avg cap of 9.19% vs our
2010 avg of 8.15% — adding them would lift our 2010 by an estimated
~80bps depending on the weighting, closing essentially the entire gap.

1. Exact `(state, address-first-number, normalized-street)` match.
2. Fuzzy fallback: same state + first number, first-2-or-3-words of
   street match.

## Shipped — R72 migration

**File**: `supabase/migrations/20260524_cm_round72_master_xlsx_sales_backfill.sql`

**Status**: file is in the repo for review; **NOT yet applied**. The
backfill writes 621 INSERTs to `sales_transactions` on the dia
Supabase project, all tagged `data_source='master_xlsx_backfill_r72'`
for clean rollback.

**Structure**: single VALUES CTE → INSERT … SELECT with a `WHERE NOT
EXISTS` idempotency guard on (property_id, sale_date, sold_price ± $100).

**Rollback**:
```sql
DELETE FROM sales_transactions WHERE data_source = 'master_xlsx_backfill_r72';
```

After this lands, the dia cap-rate charts (Cap_Avg, Returns_Idx,
Cost_Capital, Cap_Quartile, etc.) should converge most of the way
toward master parity. The 379 unmatched-no-property sales would close
the remainder; deferred to a separate round that needs property-

## Deferred — sentiment pre-2014 backfill

Same investigation also extracted master's listing-side columns
(INITIAL PRICE / INITIAL CAP / LAST PRICE / LAST CAP). For pre-2014
there are **53 master sales** with both initial and last prices/caps —
enough to add ~7-9 records per year to `available_listings` for
2007-2013. After R72 sales backfill lands, a follow-up R73 can:

1. For each master row with listing data, locate the matching
   sales_transaction_id (either pre-existing or newly created by R72).
2. INSERT an `available_listings` row with initial_price, last_price,
   initial_cap_rate, last_cap_rate, sold_date, sale_transaction_id.
3. Tag `notes='master_xlsx_listings_backfill_r73'` for rollback.

Projected impact on `seller_sentiment`: the chart's data-aware
MIN_YEAR (R70, threshold n_all≥5) would extend the chart back from
~Q3 2014 to roughly ~2011-2012, depending on how many of the 53 land
in the same TTM window. The remaining pre-2011 sparseness reflects
genuine absence of listing-side data — those sales never went through
a public listing process or pre-date our ingestion.

R73 is deferred because it depends on R72 sale_ids being assigned by
the database insert.

## Out of scope for this round

- Creating 379 NEW property records to host master's other unmatched
  sales. Requires address geocoding, deduplication checks against
  existing properties, and source attribution. Tractable but a
  separate batch.

- Re-running the matching with a tighter price tolerance (currently
  $1K rounding). Some master rows have prices like $4,907,000 that
  rounded match our $4,500,000 — they probably are NOT the same sale.
  A $5K or $10K tolerance would reduce false positives but might
  miss real matches with data-entry differences. Default $1K is the
  permissive end of the trade-off.

- Tracing why the 1,040 master sales aren't in our `sales_transactions`
  to begin with. Likely sources: pre-CoStar ingestion ingested only a
  subset of dia sales; some sales are at properties we never created;
  some are in old broker memo notes that never went through automated
  ingestion. Reconciliation with each source is its own project.
