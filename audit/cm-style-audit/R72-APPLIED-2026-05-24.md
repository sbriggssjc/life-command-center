# R72 — Applied: 532 sales backfilled, 80bps gap meaningfully closed

R72's migration file landed in main via PR. This doc records the actual
application against the dia Supabase project plus the post-backfill
parity audit.

## Application

- **Method**: chunked execute_sql via MCP (one CTE+INSERT per ~100 rows).
- **Dedup guards in the SQL**:
  - `WHERE NOT EXISTS` against `(property_id, sale_date, COALESCE(sold_price, 0))` (matches the partial unique index `sales_property_date_price_uidx`).
  - `WHERE NOT EXISTS` against the generated `dedup_natural_key` of `(property_id, lpad(round(price/1000)*1000, 12, '0'), YYYY-MM)` (matches the partial unique index `ux_sales_transactions_dedup_live`).
  - Python-side pre-dedup eliminated 10 within-batch duplicates from master that would have hit both indexes.
- **Result**: 532 of 621 candidate rows inserted; 89 skipped by the
  dedup guards (mostly date-collisions or near-duplicates of sales we
  already had).
- **All rows tagged**: `data_source = 'master_xlsx_backfill_r72'` for
  one-line rollback (`DELETE WHERE data_source = '...'`).
- **Date span**: 1997-10-23 to 2026-01-13.

## Remaining open items

- **419 unmatched master sales without a matching property** in our DB —
  these need property records created first. Tractable but a separate
  batch; would close most of the remaining 25-90bps 2010 gap and lift
  recent (2020+) sample counts further.

- **R73 sentiment backfill** — 53 master sales pre-2014 carry
  initial/last price + initial/last cap data that can populate
  `available_listings` for the seller_sentiment chart, extending it
  back ~3-4 years from the current Q3-2014 effective start. Now
  unblocked since R72 sale_ids exist.

- **80bps gap is not "0bps"** — even with 532 new sales the master
  values remain modestly higher in 2009-2010 (where master also has
  more sales pre-existing). The remaining gap is mostly the 419
  unmatched master sales the R72 batch couldn't address.
