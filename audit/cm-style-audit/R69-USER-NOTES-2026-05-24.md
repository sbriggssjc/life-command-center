## Investigation

### Finding 1: dia 2005 IS in the 4s — but only because of a single sale

| period_end | TTM avg cap | n (TTM) |
| --- | ---: | ---: |
| 2005-01-31 | **4.73%** | 9 |
| 2005-02-28 | 4.73% | 9 |
| 2005-03-31 | 4.73% | 9 |
| 2005-04-30 | 5.49% | 10 |
| 2005-07-31 | 6.06% | 14 |
| 2005-12-31 | 6.43% | 12 |
| 2006-12-31 | 7.86% | 16 |

The "4.73%" we show for Jan-Mar 2005 comes from **one** sale on
2004-08-13 ($300K, stated_cap_rate 4.73%) plus 8 other 2004 sales. As
new 2005 sales enter the TTM and the 2004 sales roll off, the average
jumps to 5-7% (real market level).

User's expectation that "2005 cap rates should be in the 4s" doesn't
match the underlying sales data — only the very-early-2005 TTM happens
to land in the 4s because of a single low-cap-rate 2004 sale dominating
a tiny TTM window.

### Finding 2: the master Excel doesn't have 2005 data at all

Audit of `Dialysis Comp Work MASTER.xlsx > Charts` tab:
- First populated row: row 15 = **Jan 2009**, ttm_cap = 8.61%, n = 18.
- No data populated in rows 3-14 (Jan-Dec 2008 are blank).
- Master's "Cap (TTM)" chart (chart15.xml) starts its series at row 23
  = **Sep-2009**.

So the user's master reference doesn't show 2005 at all. The chart they
remember from the master starts at 2009, with 2009 values in the
**8.6-9.2% range** — nowhere near 4%.

### Finding 3: ~80bps methodology gap (master 8.6-9.2% vs ours 7.7-8.1% in 2009)

Even after the 4-12% sanity filter our view already applies, our Jan-2009
TTM is 7.72% (n=20-23) vs master's 8.61% (n=18). The smaller master
sample means master excludes 2-5 sales we include — root cause not yet
identified. Candidates:
- Master may exclude portfolio / leasehold / distressed sales we include.
- Master's `SOLD CAP` column may differ slightly from our `COALESCE(calculated_cap_rate, stated_cap_rate, cap_rate)`.
- Master may have manually-curated comps that don't fully overlap with
  our `sales_transactions` universe.

This 80bps gap is a follow-up data-source reconciliation item — not
addressable in the chart layer.

## Shipped

### Data-aware MIN_YEAR cutoff for three cap-rate-bearing charts

`MIN_YEAR_BY_TEMPLATE` entries can now be either a static year (R47
behavior) or a function `(rows) => year`. The three user-named charts —
`cap_rate_ttm_by_quarter`, `cash_leveraged_returns`, `cost_of_capital`
— switched to a function that walks the row data and returns the first
year where 4+ consecutive months have `transaction_count_ttm ≥ 15`.
Falls back to 2009 (master parity) when row data is missing.

**Per-vertical effective cutoffs:**
- **dia**: ~2009 (first year with sustained n ≥ 15 — see Finding 1 above).
- **gov**: ~2005 (n=29-45 from the start of the time series; no trim
  applied because gov's pre-2009 data is dense and credible).

The cutoff is no longer "the same for all verticals", which would have
unnecessarily trimmed gov's valid 2005-2008 cap-rate history.

**Code**: new helper `findFirstDenseYear(rows, fieldKey, minN, consecutive)`
in `api/_shared/cm-native-chart-injector.js`. Threshold of n=15 picked
to match what looks credible for TTM cap-rate averages — fewer than 15
sales in the trailing 12 months produces single-sample outlier-driven
TTMs (see early 2005 dia behavior above).
